The Importance of Coral Reefs

Introduction to Coral Reefs

Coral Reefs: Underwater Wonders

Coral reefs are living structures, built by tiny animals called coral polyps. These polyps create hard skeletons that form the reef, home to a quarter of all marine life. They thrive in warm, shallow waters, thanks to a partnership with algae that provide food and color.

Why They Matter:

Reefs protect coastlines, support fisheries, and boost tourism. But they're under threat from climate change, pollution, and overfishing. Rising temperatures cause coral bleaching, weakening them. We must act now to protect these vital ecosystems.
