The Importance of Coral Reefs

Why Are Coral Reefs Important?

Coral reefs are vital for marine life, coastal protection, and human communities. They support 25% of marine species, act as natural barriers against storms, and provide food and income for millions. Reefs also contribute to medicine, tourism, and ocean health. However, they face threats from climate change, pollution, and overfishing, making their protection crucial.
